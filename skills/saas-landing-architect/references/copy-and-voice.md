# Copy & Voice

How the winning pages write. Formulas first, then voice, then a CTA bank — all with
verbatim examples from the teardown.

## The hero headline — 8 proven formulas

The headline carries 80% of the page. Draft 5–8, then choose. Always lead with the
**outcome the user gets**, not what the product is.

1. **Triadic command** — three short imperatives, often "+ AI".
   - "Measure. Reduce. Report. With AI." (LowrCarbon)
   - "Automate. Engage. Convert. Powered by AI." (Alter)
   - "Spend less. Save more. Stress less." (Lunera)
   - Rhythm is the point. Best for a punchy, confident brand.

2. **Verb + outcome** — start with an action, end with the payoff.
   - "Access all your AI tools in a single platform" (Momentum)
   - "Launch your SaaS tool, smarter and faster" (ReadyLaunch)
   - "Track every dollar, grow every investment." (Lunera)

3. **Take control / empower** — agency to the user.
   - "Take control of your daily Workflows" (Worklane; last word animates/rotates)
   - "Empower Your Business with Custom AI Solutions" (Nexilo)

4. **Outcome without the pain** — name the relief.
   - "Run your freelance business like a pro" + "without the chaos" (Dreelio)
   - "...converts—without writing a single line of code" (ReadyLaunch subhead)

5. **Negation → elevation** — reject the commodity framing.
   - "We don't make apps. We build category winners." (MVN)

6. **Conversational opener** — warm, human.
   - "Say hello to smarter hiring" (Remote)
   - "Work Smarter With AI" (LynAI)

7. **Delegation / relief** — the product does it *for you*.
   - "AI that organizes your day for you." (AppDrop)
   - "Echo will organise your lost context" (Echo)

8. **Reframe the role** (high-ticket) — name the buyer's hidden problem.
   - "We run the operations so you can run the studio" (TINKR)
   - "Your operations, reinvented by AI." (Machina)

## The subhead — the job

One sentence. It must do three things: (a) expand the promise concretely, (b) say
who it's for, (c) hint at the mechanism. 12–25 words.
- "A hiring platform that works the way you do." (flattery + fit)
- "Stop juggling tools. Keep work, clients, and cash flow in one place so your team
  always knows what to do next." (problem + consolidation + outcome — Worklane)
- "Every email, call, and Slack thread tied to one deal view. Your team sells with
  context, not guesswork." (Echo)

## Recurring micro-formulas (use throughout the page)

- **Consolidation rule-of-three:** name the three things you unify and repeat them.
  "tasks, clients, and revenue" (Worklane); "tasks, reminders, and calendar" (AppDrop);
  "clients, projects, and payments" (Dreelio). Reuse the exact triad in the final CTA.
- **"Stop X."** problem→solution. "Stop juggling tools." / "Stop piecing together
  emails, notes, and Slack threads."
- **"Say goodbye to X."** pain removal. "Say goodbye to endless searching and waiting."
- **"[Benefit], none of the [pain]."** "All the features, none of the headaches."
- **"[Outcome] (and less [secondary pain])."** "Three steps to less carbon (and less
  stress)." Parenthetical asides add wit and humanity.
- **Antithesis pairs.** "Less paperwork, more people work." / "Better systems. Less
  chaos." / "From chaos to clarity."
- **Eyebrow + benefit headline.** Lowercase category eyebrow over a bold benefit line:
  eyebrow "real-time updates" → headline "Empowering your talent pipeline" (Remote).
- **Personify the product** (AI especially). "Your AI Workforce, Always On" (LynAI);
  "It's like having a senior associate who never sleeps" (Machina testimonial).
- **Feature naming.** Smart/AI + noun: Smart Capture, AI Scheduling, Smart Reminders,
  AI Insights. Trademark a hero feature: "Smart Save™", "Growth Score".
- **Validation framing.** "Made for how your team actually works." — signals you
  understand their reality.

## Section headline patterns (not the hero)

- "Everything you need, nothing you don't" / "Everything you need in one place"
- "Built for [X], powered by [Y]" — "Built for freelancers, powered by simplicity"
- "[Quality] thinking, [quality] execution" — "Product thinking, pixel perfect execution"
- "Simple plans for serious work" / "Straightforward pricing. No hidden complexity."
- "Loved by people who hate [the chore]" — "Loved by people who hate carbon math"

## Tone of voice — the default and the dials

**Default SaaS voice:** confident, plain-spoken, benefit-led, reassuring, anti-friction.
Short sentences. Second person ("you/your"). Verbs over nouns. No jargon in headlines.

**Dials you can turn** (pick one personality, hold it consistently):
- **Witty / irreverent** (LowrCarbon): "Because saving the planet shouldn't feel like
  doing your taxes." Use self-aware parentheticals. Great for crowded/boring categories.
- **Calm / premium** (AppDrop, Lunera, Fragments): "Built for human thinking.
  Automated Intelligently." Spare, lots of whitespace, understated.
- **Bold / contrarian** (MVN): "We don't make apps. We build category winners."
- **Founder-to-founder empathy** (TINKR): "You didn't start a studio to become a
  people manager." Names the emotional pain directly.
- **Enterprise-authoritative** (Machina, Nexilo, Alter): credentials + compliance +
  ROI numbers. "Led by AI engineers from multinational corporations."

**Avoid:** generic hype with no specifics ("revolutionary platform", "next-gen
solution") unless backed by a number or a concrete mechanism. The weak templates lean
on buzzwords; the strong ones lean on outcomes.

## CTA language bank (verbatim, by intent)

- **Self-serve start (primary, P):** "Start for free", "Try for free", "Start free
  trial", "Get started", "Create Free Account", "Download App".
- **Lower-commitment secondary:** "See features", "Watch demo", "See how it works",
  "Learn more", "Book a demo", "Get a demo".
- **High-ticket primary (G):** "Book a call", "Book a free 30-min intro call",
  "Get Your Free AI Audit", "Discuss your ideas", "Scope your project", "Contact sales".
- **Soft commit:** "Join waitlist", "Join the waitlist".
- **Pricing card CTA:** match the tier — "Start for free" (free), "Get started" (mid),
  "Contact sales" (enterprise).

**CTA rules:** exactly ONE primary visual style per page (one accent color, one weight).
The primary CTA repeats 3–5×: hero, mid-page, pricing, final band. Secondary CTA is
always lower-commitment than primary. Verbs only — never "Submit" or "Click here".
