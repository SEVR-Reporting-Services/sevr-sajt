# Swedish-Language Adaptation

Rules for porting these patterns into Swedish B2B/SaaS copy. Swedish converts best when
it sounds like a competent Swedish colleague — not a translated American sales page.

## Voice principles (lagom, not hype)
- **Du-form, always.** Address the reader as "du/dig/din". Never the formal "Ni".
- **Lagom tone.** Swedish readers distrust superlatives and exclamation marks. Replace
  "revolutionary / world-class / amazing" with concrete, understated claims. Confidence
  comes from specifics, not adjectives.
- **Plain Swedish (klarspråk).** Short sentences, everyday words, active voice. If a
  sentence sounds like a press release, rewrite it.
- **Trust over excitement.** Calm competence outsells enthusiasm in Scandinavian B2B.
- **Sparing emphasis.** Few bold words, almost no exclamation points, no emoji unless
  the brand is deliberately playful.

## What to keep in English
Some terms read as natural in Swedish tech and shouldn't be translated:
- Keep: **SaaS, dashboard, demo, app, team** (often), **AI, workflow** (or "flöde"),
  **onboarding** (or "kom igång"), **insights** → usually translate to "insikter".
- Translate: features → **funktioner**, pricing → **priser/prissättning**, free trial
  → **gratis testperiod / prova gratis**, get started → **kom igång**, sign up →
  **skapa konto / registrera dig**, learn more → **läs mer**.
Judge by what a Swedish product team actually says in standup, not by the dictionary.

## CTA bank (Swedish)
- Primary start: **"Kom igång"**, **"Kom igång gratis"**, **"Prova gratis"**, **"Testa
  gratis"**, **"Skapa konto"**, **"Starta din testperiod"**.
- Secondary: **"Boka demo"**, **"Se hur det fungerar"**, **"Läs mer"**, **"Se
  funktioner"**, **"Boka ett samtal"**.
- High-ticket: **"Boka ett möte"**, **"Boka en kostnadsfri genomgång"**, **"Prata med
  oss"**, **"Kontakta sälj"**.

## Risk-reversal bank (Swedish)
- **"Gratis i 14 dagar."**
- **"Inget kort krävs."** / **"Inga kortuppgifter."**
- **"Avsluta när du vill."** (cancel anytime)
- **"Kom igång på 2 minuter."**
- **"30 dagars pengarna-tillbaka-garanti."**
- **"Ingen bindningstid."**

## Headline formulas in Swedish
The English formulas port well; keep the rhythm, lose the hype.
- Triadic command: "Mät. Minska. Rapportera." / "Spendera mindre. Spara mer. Stressa
  mindre."
- Verb + outcome: "Få koll på hela din verksamhet på ett ställe."
- Take control: "Ta kontroll över din vardag." / "Ta kontroll över dina flöden."
- Stop the pain: "Sluta jonglera verktyg." → "Samla arbete, kunder och kassaflöde på ett
  ställe."
- Consolidation triad: name the three things in Swedish and reuse them in the final CTA
  ("uppgifter, kunder och fakturor").
- Conversational: "Säg hej till smartare [X]." (use lightly — can read as too cute).

## Section labels (Swedish)
- Funktioner · Så fungerar det · Priser · Vanliga frågor (FAQ) · Kundcase / Referenser ·
  Integrationer · Om oss · Boka demo · Säkerhet.
- Eyebrows stay short and lowercase: "så fungerar det", "för team", "realtidsdata".

## Common pitfalls
- **Don't translate idioms literally.** "We've got your back" ≠ "vi har din rygg" →
  use "vi finns där hela vägen" or "vi har koll så slipper du".
- **Avoid Swenglish clutter.** Don't stack English buzzwords; pick the Swedish word when
  one exists and is natural.
- **Numbers & currency:** Swedish uses space as thousands separator and comma decimal
  (1 234,56), "kr" or "SEK", and "kr/mån" for monthly. Write "19 kr/kund" not "19kr".
- **Compound nouns:** Swedish compounds are one word — "kundregister", "kassaflöde",
  "testperiod". Splitting them ("kund register") is a classic error and looks unprofessional.
- **Capitalization:** Swedish does NOT title-case headlines. "Kom igång gratis", not
  "Kom Igång Gratis". Only capitalize the first word and proper nouns.
