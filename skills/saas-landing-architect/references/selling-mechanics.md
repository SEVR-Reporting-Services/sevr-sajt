# Selling Mechanics

The conversion levers. Most weak pages have every section but none of these mechanics.
Layer them deliberately.

## 1. Social-proof escalation
Numbers should GROW as the visitor scrolls — it builds momentum.
- Near hero: a rating or count ("4.8/5.0 — 356 reviews", "40K+ users", "Trusted by
  120+ businesses", "4.9/5 from 3,470 customers").
- After hero: logo wall ("16,000+ SMBs trust Remote").
- Mid-page: stats band ("123,000+ businesses").
- Footer: the biggest number ("Join the 40,000+ businesses using Remote"). Remote
  literally goes 16,000 → 40,000 top to bottom.
Place at least one proof element above the fold, always.

## 2. Proof-by-mockup (show, don't tell)
Every capability claim is paired with a UI screenshot — dashboards, pipelines, charts,
phone screens. The product visual IS the proof. Never make a feature claim without a
visual beside it. Apps show phone mockups; SaaS shows dashboards; agencies show
portfolio work and case-study results.

## 3. Quantified testimonials
A testimonial without a number is decoration. The strong ones carry a metric and a
real role + company:
- "...reduce manual work by over 60%" (LynAI)
- "cut emissions 22% in six months" (LowrCarbon)
- "+70% reduction in manual work", "2x faster delivery" (Nexilo)
- "We closed two enterprise deals faster because Echo flagged stalled momentum" (Echo)
Lead the quote with an emotional/outcome hook ("Replaced three tools at once"), then
the substance. Use named person + title + company + photo. Borrow authority with
recognizable logos where genuine (Dreelio cites Meta, Google).

## 4. Price anchoring (the pricing section is engineered)
- **Three tiers**, middle one badged "Most popular" / "Best value" and visually lifted
  (taller, accent border). The eye lands on it; it's the intended default.
- **Monthly/Yearly toggle** with "Save 20%"/"Save 30%" — makes annual feel smart and
  reframes the monthly number as the expensive option.
- **"Everything in [lower tier], plus:"** stacking makes higher tiers feel additive.
- **Top tier "Custom / Contact sales"** with no price = anchor + enterprise lead-gen.
  Removing the number makes the visible numbers feel reasonable.
- **Freemium** (AppDrop, Lunera, Dreelio): Free tier removes the entry barrier; Pro
  is the real target. "Save 20% on yearly."
- **Pay-once** (Fragments): "$24 Basic" makes "$99 Advanced" feel premium-justified;
  "Pay once, use forever. No subscription fees" is the whole pitch.
- **High-ticket** (Machina "Starts at $50K", TINKR audit €1,499 credited to retainer):
  tiers map to commitment stage — validate → scale → mission-critical.

## 5. Risk reversal (repeat it near every CTA)
The single most under-used lever. Place it under the hero CTA, in pricing, and in the
final band:
- "Free for 14 days. No card required." (Worklane — repeated 3×)
- "No credit card required." (Dreelio, LynAI)
- "Sign-up in 2 mins." / "No card needed." (LowrCarbon)
- "30-day money-back guarantee" (Fragments)
- "Get Your Free AI Audit" / "before you commit to anything" (Machina)
- "15 mins free. No pressure, just a thoughtful chat." (Agnos)
- Audit fee credited toward retainer within 30 days (TINKR).

## 6. Problem agitation + us-vs-them
Name the pain before selling the cure. Two devices:
- **Agitation block:** "you became the bottleneck in your own business" (TINKR), an
  explicit "The Challenge" section (AppDrop), "Stop juggling tools" (Worklane).
- **Comparison table:** "Other agencies" vs "Agnos"; "The usual fix" vs "TINKR";
  "Human vs AI Performance". Lists the buyer's specific pains in the loser column.
Strongest on high-ticket and crowded-category pages.

## 7. Effort reduction
Lower the perceived work of adopting:
- 3–4 step "how it works" ("just three simple steps").
- "without writing a single line of code".
- "Let AI handle the rest." (AppDrop).
- Integrations grid ("works with the tools you already use") = no rip-and-replace.

## 8. Scarcity & urgency (use sparingly, only if true)
- "2 spots available for partnership" / "START HERE" (TINKR).
- "Available for project" status dot (Agnos).
- Release/funding announcement bars create a sense of momentum.
Only deploy real scarcity — fake countdowns erode trust.

## 9. Authority & trust badges
- Credentials: "Led by AI engineers from multinational corporations" (Machina).
- Compliance: SOC 2, HIPAA, 99.9% SLA badges (Machina), "enterprise-grade security"
  footer block (Worklane).
- Funding: "Announcing our $2.3M Seed Round" (Remote), "We just raised seed funding".
- Founder presence: video/quote + named CEO at the close (Agnos, Nexilo, MVN).
- Cause signal: "We donate 2% of your membership to pediatric wellbeing" (Alter).

## 10. Segmentation / self-identification
Use-case rows and per-industry cards let the visitor find themselves ("Agencies,
Consultants, Startups, Freelancers..."; 4 industry cards with tailored compliance
bullets). Relevance increases conversion.

---

## How to layer them — the persuasion stack
A complete page runs this emotional sequence:
**Desire** (hero promise) → **Belief** (logo wall, near-hero proof) → **Pain**
(problem) → **Understanding** (feature→benefit + mockups) → **Confidence** (how it
works, integrations) → **Trust** (stats, quantified testimonials, badges) →
**Decision** (anchored pricing) → **Reassurance** (FAQ, risk reversal) →
**Action** (final CTA). If a page feels flat, it's usually missing Pain, quantified
Trust, or Reassurance — not sections, but mechanics.

## Market-leader notes (Slack / Linear / Stripe / Notion / Vercel)
- **One** crisp value proposition, said plainly. Slack: "Where work happens." Specific
  "made for [audience]" beats generic "for everyone".
- Product UI is the hero asset, shown in motion/interaction, not a stock photo.
- Customer logos above the fold; outcome stats ("X% faster") over adjectives.
- A single dominant CTA color used nowhere else, so the eye always finds the next step.
- Restraint: heavy whitespace, one accent color, tight type scale. Premium = calm.
- Scroll-triggered reveals and subtle motion guide attention without gimmicks.
- Specificity wins: real numbers, real customer names, real screenshots. Every
  placeholder or vague claim is a trust leak.
