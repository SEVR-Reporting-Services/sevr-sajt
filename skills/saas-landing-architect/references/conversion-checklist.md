# Conversion Checklist — 25-point pre-launch audit

Run every page through this before delivering. Fix every miss. Grouped by zone.

## Above the fold (the 5-second test)
1. Can a stranger tell WHAT this is and WHO it's for within 5 seconds? Headline states
   an outcome, not a category.
2. Subhead names the audience and the mechanism in one sentence.
3. Exactly one primary CTA, visually dominant (the only use of the accent color).
4. A secondary, lower-commitment CTA is present (demo/see-how).
5. At least one proof element is above the fold (rating, user count, or logos).
6. The product is shown, not described — a real UI mockup/screenshot/phone frame.
7. Risk reversal sits under the hero CTA for self-serve ("No card required").

## Proof & trust
8. Logo wall or trust number immediately after the hero.
9. Social proof escalates down the page (numbers grow, not repeat identically).
10. Testimonials are named (person + role + company + photo), not anonymous.
11. At least half the testimonials contain a specific number or outcome.
12. NO placeholder stats anywhere ("0K+", "0.0", "$0M", lorem ipsum). This is the #1
    unfinished-template tell.
13. Trust/security signals present if relevant (badges, compliance, funding).

## Body & persuasion
14. Every feature is framed as a benefit (headline = outcome, feature name in body).
15. Each major claim is paired with a supporting visual.
16. The page names a problem/pain before selling the solution.
17. A "how it works" (3–4 steps) removes the "is this hard?" objection.
18. A single, consistent voice/personality throughout (not generic buzzwords).

## Pricing
19. Three tiers (or deliberate freemium/pay-once), with a clearly anchored middle tier
    badged "Most popular".
20. Monthly/Yearly toggle with a visible "Save X%" (for subscriptions).
21. Each tier states who it's for in one line; higher tiers stack "Everything in X, plus".
22. The pricing CTA language matches the tier (free → "Start free"; top → "Contact sales").

## Close & hygiene
23. FAQ answers real objections (security, integration, ROI, switching), not template meta.
24. Final CTA band restates the promise + repeats risk reversal.
25. Mobile (380px): headline doesn't overflow, CTA is thumb-reachable, proof stays
    above the fold, all multi-column layouts stack cleanly, motion respects
    `prefers-reduced-motion`.

## Scoring
- 23–25 ✅ ship it.
- 18–22 ⚠️ fixable gaps — address misses before launch.
- < 18 ❌ structural problems — likely missing proof, a clear CTA, or benefit framing.

## The three most common failures (fix these first)
1. **Feature-led headlines** instead of outcome-led ("AI recommendation engine" vs
   "Know your next move"). → Rewrite every headline as the user's payoff.
2. **No risk reversal / weak CTA** — buried or multiple competing CTAs. → One primary,
   repeated, with "No card required" beside it.
3. **Placeholder/vague proof** — demo zeros, anonymous quotes, no logos. → Replace with
   real numbers, named customers, and visible logos, or remove the section.
